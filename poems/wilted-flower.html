The Wilted Flower

July 16, 2026 1 min read Poem
◌ ◌ ◌

Planted firmly in the ground.

Reaching beyond the skies.

Though roots keep me tightly bound,

one should remain wise.

I'm radiant above the sun,

captivating every eye.

Here while my days are spun.

I dread the last goodbye.

Euphoria won't forever last.

It cannot live hereafter.

So cherish each moment while it lasts.

Or regret will follow after.

One must learn how to accept.

Even the brightest petal falls.

Though cherished memories are kept.

Time outlives them all.
