Eccentric Feeling

“Live, feel, and be free.”

July 4, 2026 3 min read Personal Essay
◌ ◌ ◌

A quiet breeze of air brushing against my face, yet it screams loudly inside my brain. I can't help but wonder how one would name such a strange feeling. A feeling so unfamiliar that it's hard to comprehend.

A normal person would sometimes ignore how they feel because they're too scared to face that unfamiliar emotion. It got me curious. Why do people choose to set aside what they really feel just to distract themselves from reality?

Isn't it fun to sit and face your authentic emotions? Or are people just too scared to find the courage to name what they feel? Well, I guess we're all different, and that's valid.

One thing I've learned during my short college vacation is that it's okay to set aside your emotions first, especially if they're too intense to contain or might end up hurting others.

But while setting them aside, you should still understand where those emotions are coming from.

“It's important that we acknowledge our feelings because it's one way to understand them.”

I'm not saying we should abandon our emotions. What I'm saying is that we should allow them to be felt, heard, or even seen.

Reality hits hard because it's difficult to deal with these kinds of things inside you, especially if you're doing it all by yourself.

But I beg to differ.

I think it's better to sort things out on your own because you're the one who's feeling them.

Seeking help is totally valid. Of course, you can always ask for help. Perhaps it's the easier thing to do.

But I do believe this is also a challenge for you.

A challenge to finally face that unfamiliar feeling. Give yourself a chance to process it and allow that eccentric feeling to be understood.

Avoiding it will only make it fill the empty void within you.

So live. Feel. Be free. Find the harmony you're meant to have.
