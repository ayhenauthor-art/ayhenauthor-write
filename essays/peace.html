The Everlasting Peace

“Peace begins with understanding yourself.”

July 20, 2026 3 min read Personal Essay
◌ ◌ ◌

At night I wonder with my mind open. Seeing bridges on wide skies connecting dreams of forever that last.

I asked myself.

Do I have what it takes to achieve a peace that lingers in my mind?

Funny how I, myself cannot define what that means.

But I know some things for sure—that I wanted that peace. Yet somehow, I remain confused.

In this world full of chaos, peace is a luxury that a baffled mind can't always afford. There are many things that threaten peace, but believing that it can be achieved never prevents us from reaching it.

I have always believed that solitude is one of the keys to achieving an everlasting peace.

Some people confuse solitude with loneliness.

But loneliness is something entirely different—a powerful emotion capable of unsettling even the healthiest mind.

For me, solitude means learning to sit with your own emotions, enjoying your own company, and discovering a quiet form of self-love.

Feeling secure is another path toward peace.

Secure in how you feel. Secure with yourself.

So you see, peace can be obtained by understanding yourself.

No one can dictate how you should feel because you alone choose how you respond to what life places before you.

I have always believed it should be that way.

Because once you learn to be with yourself, you'll always be with the everlasting peace.
