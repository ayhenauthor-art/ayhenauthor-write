Blizzard of Judgement

July 16, 2026 3 min read Personal Essay
◌ ◌ ◌

A person who has a finger in every pie is someone who is lonely or full of hatred—not because they like to meddle with other people's affairs, but because they're insecure.

What a vile accusation.

Personally, I do believe in the good of others. But understanding an individual is a different kind of story. Knowing a person requires more than observation. It requires a genuine connection.

Sometimes, beneath scattered noon clouds, I wonder about the thoughts of strangers. They can be good or bad. I think of their judgement not because I find myself lacking, but because I'm curious about someone's mindset.

I'm curious about how they think, act, or even make heavy decisions. Having the chance to truly know a person is fascinating. You become an investigator searching for answers about what they love and what they fear.

Occasionally, I empty my own plate just to fill someone else's bowl. I tend to give myself completely whenever I befriend someone. That's where it always goes wrong. I choose their convenience over mine for the sake of what I believed was friendship.

There came moments when I quietly asked myself,

“Is this really a friendship?”

That question became the sign to finally start listening—not to the noise around me, but to myself.

Listen to your own thoughts. Listen to your own emotions. Allow them to be felt.

Perhaps judgement is not something to fear, but something to sharpen. Trust someone only if they have proven themselves trustworthy.

I choose to carry a blizzard of judgement, because at the end of the day, the person who stays with me the longest is myself.
